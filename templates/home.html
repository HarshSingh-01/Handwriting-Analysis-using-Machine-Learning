Abstract

Handwriting Analysis or Graphology is the science of identifying, evaluating and revealing the personality of an individual through patterns and strokes hidden in the handwriting. It includes examining the features such as size of letters and words, spacing between the letters and words, pen pressure, margins, slant angle. Graphology

Graphology or Handwriting analysis is defined as the examination of the physical characteristics and handwriting patterns in order to understand the cognitive patterns and the state of mind of a person. The handwriting of a person is used to identify, evaluate, and understand the emotional state and temperament of a person. Graphologists or Handwriting experts identify strokes, spaces, patterns, margins, pressure etc. to predict the personality traits of a person which are represented by neurological brain patterns. These brain patterns contribute exceptional neuromuscular movements which are common to everyone with the inherited personality trait. These handwriting patterns reflect the subconscious state of mind of an individual.

Applications

  • Personal Relationships

    Handwriting is analyzed to check compatibility in all types of personal relationships. Where family members are incompatible or find themselves in situations of conflict, an analysis of their handwritings will highlight the areas where personality clashes occur.

  • In Education

    Handwriting Analysis is valuable in the field of education and in the counselling of students. It is particularly useful where interaction between teachers and pupils or between the pupils themselves is problematic.

    Children under-achieve because of low self-esteem and other personality problems. An analysis of handwriting can help us to discover and encourage latent skills or talents that might have gone unnoticed.

  • Job Applications

    For job seekers handwriting analysis provides an added bonus in that it requires no painful or embarrassing interviews which a sensitive candidate is likely to see as an invasion of privacy.

    Because of nervousness, an otherwise perfectly qualified applicant may not be able to do justice to an interview. As a result, the employer may end up with a completely incorrect assessment of the applicant's suitability for the post.
